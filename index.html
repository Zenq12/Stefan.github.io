Early origins

Football, known as soccer in some places, has ancient roots:

China: Around 200 BC, the Chinese played "Cuju," kicking a leather ball into a small net. Greece and Rome: Ball games were used for physical training.


Modern football

Football as we know it began in England in the 19th century:

1863: The Football Association (FA) in England created the first official rules.

1888: The first professional league, The Football League, was formed in England.


Global Spread

Football quickly became popular worldwide:

1904: FIFA (Fédération Internationale de Football Association) was founded.

1930: The first World Cup was held in Uruguay, with the host nation winning.


Modern Changes

Football has evolved with new tactics and technology:

Technology: VAR (Video Assistant Referee) helps make decisions on the field.

Tactics: Teams use data analysis for better performance.


World Cup

The FIFA World Cup, often simply called the World Cup, is an international association football competition among the senior men's national teams of the members of the Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA), the sport's global governing body.

UEFA European Championship

The UEFA European Football Championship,[1] less formally the European Championship and informally the Euros,[2][3] is the primary association football tournament organised by the Union of European Football Associations (UEFA). The competition is contested by UEFA members' senior men's national teams, determining the continental champion of Europe.[4][5] It is the second-most watched football tournament in the world after the FIFA World Cup; the Euro 2016 final was watched by a global audience of around 600 million.[6] The competition has been held every four years since 1960,[7][8][9] except for 2020, when it was postponed until 2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic in Europe, but kept the name Euro 2020. Scheduled to be in the even-numbered year between FIFA World Cup tournaments, it was originally called the European Nations' Cup before changing to its current name in 1968. Since 1996, the individual events have been branded as "UEFA Euro [year]".


Copa America

The CONMEBOL Copa América (literally America Cup, known until 1975 as the South American Football Championship[1]), commonly shortened to Copa América, is the top men's football tournament contested among national teams from South America and North America. It is the oldest still-running continental football competition, as well as the third most watched in the world.[2] The competition determines the champions of South America.[2][3][4] Since the 1990s, teams from North America and Asia have also been invited to compete.
